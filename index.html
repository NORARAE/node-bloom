the open web → the index → the citation

The new answer engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude — don't rank a list of links; they cite a few trusted sources inside the answer.

But they mostly don't crawl alone — they ride the classic indexes. Bing feeds ChatGPT, Google feeds Gemini, Brave feeds Claude (Perplexity runs its own). So you must be crawlable & indexed first — then EEAT & authority, schema, and FAQ structure decide who gets cited.

the players
indexes — Google · Bing · Brave: the crawlers that index the web
answer engines — ChatGPT · Perplexity · Gemini · Claude: they cite sources
what you're watching

Indexes sweep outward across the web; the pages they reach become indexed (discoverable). When an engine cites a source, a gold beam travels back through its index to the engine — the source flares, crystallizes, and its gem grows a little with every citation.

cyan ripple — an index (Google · Bing · Brave) crawling the web
ringed dot — an indexed source: discoverable, not yet cited
gem in a cool ring — an engine cited it — now indexed + cited, gaining authority as it grows
gold beam + flare — a citation in motion: a cited source beams back through its index to the engine and flares the moment it lands

Why it drifts: linked sources spring together while every source gently repels the rest — so the web self-organizes into clusters, the way a real knowledge graph groups related pages.
